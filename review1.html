2019 Mercedes-Benz C Class Sedan Review

By Peter Kobasa, 2/1/2021

Biggest Takeaway

To be brutally honest, the 2018 X3 M40i is the best vehicle that I have ever driven. Of the vehicles I have reviewed thus far, the X3 M40i combines the sportiness of a BMW M235i with the roominess of a Honda Pilot, carefully precisioned into one opulent, aggressive mid-size SUV. Aside from just great throttle response and power, the X3 handles corners like a sports car, and surrounds the driver with a lavish, high-quality leather interior. Personally, I believe this vehicle is what catalyzed my interest in the BMW X6 (a larger, weirder-looking Beamer SUV with a V8), as this vehicle is a great combination of sportiness and practicality, deserving the tie for the highest scoring vehicle on the lemon list.

Some Drawbacks

While the X3 delivers an incredible and riveting driving experience, the M40i carries a hefty price tag while new (around $60,000) as this is the closest thing one can get to an X3M. Coupled with this heavy price tag is the inevitable depreciation, as a 2018 model can slip to the high 30s-low 40s range depending on the mileage. While this aspect may be a plus to those searching for a pre-owned ride, a loss worth ⅓ of the car’s original value in less than three years can be a turnoff to those in the market. Beyond this, BMW’s do not have a stellar track record in terms of reliability. While the X3 M40i possesses Beamer’s 3.0L 6 cylinder opposed to their 4.4L V8’s, the X3’s thrilling driving capabilities might be coupled with “enjoy at your own financial risk.”

Some Oddities

A few weird/interesting features about the X3 include the “seatbelt hug,” where the seatbelt oddly squeezes you to ensure your seatbelt is fastened once you put the car in drive. I’m sure you get used to this after a while but I still find it strange. For a “midsize” SUV, it looks and feels much larger than a midsize. When my friend first showed me his X3, I legitimately thought it was an X5, just because it appeared so large. Aside from that, the X3 has one of the meanest exhaust notes I have ever heard, resembling an exhaust note more like a Dodge Charger Scat Pack than a luxury family cruiser. Beyond this, the X3’s panoramic sunroof and the hood prop rod to hold up the undercarriage storage in the trunk of the vehicle are some unique touches, but go along nicely with the X3’s lavishly aggressive atmosphere.
